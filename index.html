Sample Writing

High-band essay

Some people argue that parents should be responsible for teaching children how to become responsible members of society, while others believe that schools are better suited for this role. Although educational institutions play an important part in children’s development, I believe that parents have a more significant influence on shaping their character and social values.

On the one hand, schools provide a structured environment in which children can acquire essential knowledge and social skills. Through standardized curricula and interaction with peers, students are exposed to diverse perspectives and learn how to cooperate with others. For example, classroom activities such as group projects and discussions help students develop teamwork and communication skills, which are necessary for contributing to society. However, schools often face limitations, such as large class sizes and limited individual attention, which can reduce their effectiveness in shaping each child’s personal values.

On the other hand, parents play a crucial role in guiding children’s moral development. From an early age, children are strongly influenced by their family environment, where they learn fundamental values such as empathy, responsibility, and respect. Unlike schools, parents are able to provide consistent guidance and individualized support tailored to their child’s needs. For instance, storytelling, daily interactions, and parental role-modelling can instil positive behaviours and attitudes. As a result, children raised in supportive family environments are more likely to develop into responsible and socially aware individuals.

In my view, while schools contribute significantly to academic and social development, the role of parents is more fundamental in shaping a child’s character. This is because values learned at home tend to have a lasting impact on behaviour and decision-making. In conclusion, although both schools and parents play important roles in educating children to become good members of society, I believe that parental influence is more decisive, as it forms the foundation of a child’s moral and social development.
